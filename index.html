The San Antonio Spurs

THE FRANCHISE

The franchise was founded in 1967 as the Dallas Chaparrals, in Texas, but in 1973, upon moving to the city of San Antonio, it was renamed the San Antonio Spurs. A franchise of the National Basketball Association, part of the Western Conference, and are among the most popular teams in the league Won their first title in 1999 and managed to win the League 5 times, in 2003/2005/2007/2014. The team achieved the taboo of staying for 20 years in the playoffs, with the same coach Greg Popovich. Greg was also in charge of the US basketball team until the year 2021.

TIM DUNCAN

Tim Duncan entered the NBA in 1997, was the first pick in the draft that year. In his first season, 1997-98, he had impressive numbers, leading the Spurs to the playoffs. Duncan was named rookie of the year. In his second season, in 1998-99, he led San Antonio to its first championship, beating Patrick Ewing's New York Knicks 4-1 and was named the Finals Most Valuable Player. Duncan would go on to win 4 more titles with the Spurs, in 2003, 2005, 2007 and 2014, being chosen as the most valuable player in 2003 and 2005. In 2016, after 19 seasons in the NBA, Tim Duncan decided to retire from the courts.

TEAM PLAYERS

For many years, the franchise has had a history of signing many foreign players. The team also featured Argentine star Manu Ginobili and Frenchman Tony Parker, who formed a trio with a lot of potential and were part of 4 titles, out of 5, for Spurs. Brazilian Tiago Splitter played in the franchise from 2010 to 2015.

LAST CHAMPIONSHIP

San Antonio's last title, in 2014, the team beat the strong Miami Heat of Lebron James and Dwayne Wade, 4 games to 1. The team had a collective game model that was marked in the history of the league. Currently, Spurs are revamping their team, with new players and it hasn't been doing well in recent years. At Spurs, a historic feat occurred, in which he had the first woman to command an NBA team, Becky Hammon.
